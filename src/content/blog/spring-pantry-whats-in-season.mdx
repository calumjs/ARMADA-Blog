Spring is the most exciting time on a specialty buyer's calendar. After the long, rich foods of winter, the list turns bright and green almost overnight. Here is what is arriving at Northwind Traders this month, and what we would put on your counter while it lasts.

## New-season olive oil

The first pressings from our southern Mediterranean growers have landed, and they are everything early-harvest oil should be — peppery, grassy, and almost fizzy on the back of the throat. This is the oil for finishing, not frying. We always tell grocers to taste it next to a six-month-old bottle; the difference sells itself.

> Early-harvest oil has a shelf life, not an eternity. Buy it bright, sell it fast, and tell your customers to do the same.

## Young goat cheeses

Kidding season means the first fresh chèvre of the year, and it is a world away from the firm, aged logs of autumn. Light, lemony, and barely set, these are best eaten within days — which makes our short lead times the whole point.

## Wild things

For a few short weeks we can get **wild garlic**, **sea kale**, and the first **forced rhubarb** from a small grower we have worked with for years. Quantities are tiny and the season is brutally short, so these go out on a first-come basis.

## Stocking the spring counter

A few combinations we have seen work well on grocers' shelves this month:

1. New-season oil paired with a crusty sourdough and a dish of flaky salt to taste.
2. Fresh chèvre next to a jar of our wildflower honey.
3. A bunch of wild garlic beside a wedge of young Alpine cheese — spring on a plate.

Everything here is on this month's seasonal list. As ever, it is here while it lasts and gone when the season turns, so order what catches your eye while it is at its best.
