This year, Northwind Traders turns **forty**. Four decades is a long time in any trade, and a small eternity in food, so we wanted to mark it by looking back at how a single market stall became the trading house it is today.

## It started with a cheese we could not stop talking about

In 1986, the whole of Northwind was one stall, one van, and a list of about a dozen lines. The first of them was a farmhouse cheese we had tasted on a buying trip and simply could not get out of our heads. We brought back as much as the van would hold, sold it in a weekend, and went back for more.

The shopkeepers who bought from us that first year asked the question that has shaped the company ever since: *what else can you find?*

## Growing without losing the thread

Forty years on, the catalogue runs to more than 1,200 lines and the makers we buy from are spread across four continents. But the model has not changed. We still:

- buy direct from the people who make the food,
- follow the seasons and the makers' calendars rather than a generic range,
- and treat the cold chain as a quality decision, not a cost line.

Some of the producers on our first list are still with us. Some of the grocers who bought from that first van are now stocking their second generation of family shopkeepers. Those relationships are the real catalogue.

## Thank you

A milestone like this is really a thank-you. To the farmhouses, roasters, and family producers who let us carry their food to new tables. To the grocers and chefs who trusted a small importer with their counters and their menus. And to the team who keep the cold chain holding and the seasonal lists flowing.

Here is to the next forty years — and to the next cheese we cannot stop talking about.
