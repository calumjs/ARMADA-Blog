Coffee is one of the most rewarding parts of our list, because a single bean can taste like a dozen different things depending on where and how it was grown. This season we are adding three new **single-origin** coffees, each bought direct from the smallholders and washing stations that produced them. Here is what is landing, and the tasting notes we would put on your shelf cards.

## Highland washed — bright and clean

From a cooperative of highland smallholders, this is a classic washed coffee: clean, crisp, and bright, with notes of green apple, jasmine, and a long citrus finish. It is the one we would hand to a customer who thinks they do not like coffee — it changes minds.

## Sun-dried natural — bold and fruity

Dried slowly on raised beds in the sun, this natural-process lot is the opposite character: heavy-bodied and fruit-forward, with blueberry, dark chocolate, and a syrupy sweetness. It makes a remarkable espresso and an even better excuse to linger.

## Honey process — the middle path

Processed with some of the fruit left on the bean, this honey-process coffee splits the difference: rounded and sweet, with notes of stone fruit, brown sugar, and a soft, lingering finish. It is the easy crowd-pleaser of the three.

## Selling single-origin

A few notes we share with grocers stocking single-origin for the first time:

- **Date the bags.** Single-origin is at its best within a few weeks of roast; freshness is the whole pitch.
- **Tell the story.** Provenance sells — the farm, the altitude, the process all matter to the customer paying a premium.
- **Sample it.** Nothing moves coffee like a cup in someone's hand. A small brew bar pays for itself.

All three are sourced direct, fully traceable, and roasted to order. They are on the list now, while this season's lots last.
