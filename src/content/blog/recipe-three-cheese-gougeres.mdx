We are often asked for recipes that show off the cheeses on our list without a lot of fuss. Gougères — light, savoury choux puffs — are our favourite answer. They use up odds and ends from the counter, they are forgiving to make, and a warm tray of them will sell more cheese than any sign ever could.

This version uses three Northwind cheeses, but the technique works with whatever you have to hand.

## What you need

- 250 ml water
- 100 g butter, cubed
- 150 g plain flour
- 4 eggs
- 60 g **Hochtal Reserve**, finely grated, for depth
- 40 g aged hard cheese, finely grated, for the savoury edge
- 40 g soft washed-rind cheese, in small pieces, folded through
- A good pinch of salt and a grind of pepper

## Method

1. Bring the water, butter, and salt to a rolling boil in a saucepan.
2. Tip in all the flour at once and beat hard with a wooden spoon until the dough comes together and leaves the sides of the pan. Keep stirring over low heat for a minute to dry it out.
3. Take it off the heat and let it cool for five minutes — too hot and the eggs will scramble.
4. Beat in the eggs one at a time. The dough will look like it is splitting; keep going and it comes back glossy and smooth.
5. Fold through the grated hard cheeses and the pieces of washed-rind. Season.
6. Spoon walnut-sized blobs onto a lined tray, leaving room to spread.
7. Bake at 200°C for 20–25 minutes until deep golden and dramatically puffed. Do not open the oven early or they will sink.

## A note on the cheese

The **Hochtal Reserve** does the heavy lifting here — its brothy, crystalline depth carries the whole puff. If you have ends of the Reserve that are too small to sell, this is exactly where they belong.

Serve them warm. They are best within an hour of baking, which is what makes them such a good in-store draw: bake a tray mid-morning, and the smell alone brings people to the counter.
