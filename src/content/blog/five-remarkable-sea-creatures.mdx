ARMADA borrows its language from the sea: a fleet of focused skills, a lookout in the crow's nest, a shipwright at the bench. So it feels only right to leave the harbour for a moment and meet some of the remarkable creatures that share those waters. The ocean is full of animals that are clever, tough, strange, enormous, or simply beautiful, and the five below each earn the word "remarkable" in their own way.

## The common octopus

For sheer intelligence in an unexpected body, few animals rival the octopus. It can open jars, solve simple puzzles, and squeeze its soft, boneless body through any gap wider than its beak. Special cells in its skin let it change colour and texture in an instant, so it can vanish against a rock one second and flash a warning the next. With a brain in its head and clusters of nerves in every arm, it is a reminder that intelligence can take shapes we are still learning to recognise.

## The blue whale

The blue whale is the largest animal known to have ever lived, larger even than the biggest dinosaurs. It can grow longer than three buses parked end to end, and its heart alone is about the size of a small car. For all that scale, it feeds on some of the smallest creatures in the sea, straining huge mouthfuls of tiny krill through comb-like plates. Its low, rumbling calls are among the loudest sounds any animal makes and can travel for great distances across the open ocean.

## The sea otter

Sea otters are proof that resilience and charm can swim together. They have the densest fur of any animal, with up to a million hairs packed into a single patch the size of a postage stamp, which keeps them warm without a layer of blubber. They use rocks as tools to crack open shellfish, and they often float on their backs in groups, sometimes holding hands so the current does not drift them apart while they sleep. By eating sea urchins, they also protect the kelp forests that shelter countless other creatures.

## The mantis shrimp

Small but spectacular, the mantis shrimp is one of the ocean's true oddities. Its eyes are among the most complex in the animal kingdom, able to see colours and kinds of light our own eyes cannot. Some species strike with club-like limbs so fast that the punch can crack a shell and even briefly boil the water around it. Wrapped in a shell that shimmers with colour, this thumb-sized hunter packs more surprises than animals many times its size.

## The seahorse

The seahorse is gentle, beautiful, and wonderfully unusual. It swims upright, fluttering a tiny fin on its back, and anchors itself to seagrass with a curling tail when it wants to rest. Each eye can move on its own, so it can watch for food and danger at the same time. Most remarkable of all, it is the father who carries the eggs: the male holds them in a pouch on his belly until the young are ready to drift out into the sea on their own.

From the clever octopus to the patient seahorse, the ocean keeps no shortage of wonders. May your own fleet sail on with the same curiosity that fills these waters.
